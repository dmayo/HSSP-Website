Course Schedule

We expect to cover the following topics in the following order. However, as the course progresses, we are happy to adjust the schedule to accommodate student interests and needs.

Download Syllabus
  1. July 6th - Problem Set 1 distributed
    2. Lecture notes
    1. Introduction to vectors
    2. Electric charge and Coulomb's Law
    3. Superposition principle
    4. Application: Historical discovery of the electric charge and its role in the development of quantum mechanics and early atomic models
  2. July 13th - Problem Set 2 distributed; Problem Set 1 due, Quiz 1
    1. Gauss's Law
    2. Electric Potential
    3. Classical Theory of Conductors
    4. Current
    5. Application: The semiconductor (introduction to band gap) and its role in the technological revolution of the past half century
  3. July 20th - Problem Set 3 distributed; Problem Set 2 due, Quiz 2
    1. Resistance and Ohm's Law
    2. Temperature Dependence of Resistance
    3. Simple Electric Circuits
    4. Parallel and series circuits
    5. Combination circuits
    6. Application: The beauty of superconductivity
  4. July 27th - Problem Set 4 distributed; Problem Set 3 due, Quiz 3
    1. Energy in electric Field
    2. Capacitance
    3. Properties of capacitors
    4. R-C circuits
    5. Application: Brief introduction to complex R-C impedance and circuits with mul-tiple voltage sources
  5. August 3rd - Proposal due; Problem Set 4 due, Quiz 4
    1. Introduction of the project
    2. Project orientation
      • Review of basic circuitry
      • Light-emitting diode (LED)
      • Photoresistor
    3. Drawing circuits
  6. August 10th - Construction of an automatic nightlight; Exam due
    1. Construction
    2. Testing
    3. Final remarks on the project
  7. August 17th - Grand Finale; Problem set 5 distributed
    1. Electric Fields in matter
    2. Polarizability
    3. Linear dielectrics
    4. Application: Alternative energy sources (solar cell, wind power, etc...)
    5. Forward-looking Final remarks

Do I have to do the psets?

Nope. But if you want to that would be cool. We highly encourage it. We have developed an online pset checker for this course and will be available soon.
