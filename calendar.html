Course Schedule

We expect to cover the following topics in the following order. However, as the course progresses, we are happy to adjust the schedule to accommodate student interests and needs.

Download Syllabus
  1. July 6th - Problem Set 1 distributed
    1. Introduction to vectors
    2. Electric charge and Coulomb's Law
    3. Superposition principle
    4. Application: Historical discovery of the electric charge and its role in the development of quantum mechanics and early atomic models
  2. July 13th - Problem Set 2 distributed; Problem Set 1 due
    1. Gauss's Law
    2. Electric Potential
    3. Classical Theory of Conductors
    4. Current
    5. Application: The semiconductor (introduction to band gap) and its role in the technological revolution of the past half century
  3. July 20th - Problem Set 3 distributed; Problem Set 2 due
    1. Resistance and Ohm's Law
    2. Temperature Dependence of Resistance
    3. Simple Electric Circuits
    4. Application: The beauty of superconductivity
  4. August 3rd - Proposal due; Problem Set 5 distributed; Problem Set 4 due
    1. Energy in electric Feld
    2. Capacitance
    3. Parallel circuits
    4. Series circuits
    5. Application: Alternative energy (battery, solar cell, etc...)
  5. July 27th - Problem Set 4 distributed; Problem Set 3 due
    1. Electric Fields in Matter
    2. Linear Dielectrics
    3. Polarizability
    4. Application: The unifcation of electric and magnetic felds
  6. August 10th - Exam distributed; Construction of Van de Graaff
    1. Introduction to Magnetism
    2. Lorentz Force Law
    3. Three Basic Types of Magnetic Materials
  7. August 17th - Grand Finale; Exam due
    1. Building the Van de Graaff
    2. Testing the Van de Graaff
    3. Final remarks (about what? who knows???)
